Healthcare Systems

The United States has a unique and complex healthcare system. Unlike other developed countries, we do not have government sponsered health insurance and while it allows Americans to have greater freedom in selecting their healthcare provider, it also drives up costs and leaves many without any form of insurance. This is a presentation I made with a classmate on the current state of the healthcare systems around the world and how they compare to the U.S.

UK providerThe National Health Service owns and operates the hospitals in the UK.

UK payerThe NHS is funded through taxes and the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) establishes cost effective protocols that dictate pricing.

UK accessAll residents of the UK are eligible for free treatment as well as those with a European Health Insurance Card.

France providerHospitals are both publically and privately owned (by non-profit and for-profit comapnies).

France payerSocial insurance is a type of public health insurance where the fund is managed by the goverment.

France accessEntire population must pay compulsory health insurance and choose a general practicioner to act as a "gate keeper."

Canada providerServices are mostly provided by private entities.

Canada payerThe Government operates publically funded health insurance that is transferred from the Canadian federal goverment to the provinces where it is subject to board regulations.

Canada accessAll legal residents of Canada are coverged and entitled to receive "insured services" without copayment.


Vet providerThe Veterans Health Administration (VHA) operates VA Medical Centers, Clinics, and Community Leaving Centers.

Vet payerThe budget is part of the United States Department of Veterans Affairs (VA).

Vet accessEligibility restricted to those who served in the active military, naval, or air service and separated under any condition other than dishonorable.

Employer providerHealth care facilities are largely owned and operated by private sector businesses

Employer payerPaid for by businesses on behalf of their employees as part of an employee benefit package

Employer accessEmployer-sponsored health insurance is the biggest form of private health insurance and covers some 60% of Americans.

Medicare accessHealth care facilities are largely owned and operated by private sector businesses

Medicare payerSocial insurance (Medicare) is a fund operated by the goverment to spread the risk and social welfare programs (Medicaid and the State Children's Health Insurance Program) provide assistance to people who can't afford it.

Medicare accessIn general, all persons over 65 who have been legal residens for at least five years are eligible forMedicare and Medicaid is a intended for those with low income

UninsuredAccording to the United States Census Bureau, in 2012 there were 48.0 million people in the US (15.4% of the population) who were without health insurance.
