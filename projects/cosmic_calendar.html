Cosmic Calendar

It is difficult to imagine time on the order of millions of years. The Universe is 13.8 billion years old and life began 3.8 billion years ago. Our species evolved 200,000 years ago. In order to contextualize these numbers and appreciate just how long it took for us to get here it's useful to construct a Cosmic Calendar that compresses the 13.8 billion year existence of the Universe into 1 year (demonstrated by Carl Sagan on his television series Cosmos). On that scale all of human history occured in the last 14 seconds before midnight on December 31st. Considering our relatively recent ability to navigate beyond our planet is it reasonable to assume that there may be other intelligent civilizations out there colonizing the galaxy? Could the answer to the Fermi paradox be that there simply has not been enough time for any species to have evolved beyond their planetary system? (For more on the Fermi paradox see my review of Stephen Webb's If the Universe is Teeming with Aliens... Where is Everybody?
