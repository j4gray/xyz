8 Years of Obama in Data

Jan 29 2017

In the current climate of alternative facts looking at the data is of the upmost importance so as to avoid being confused or gaslit when it comes to quantifiable and agreed upon statistics. For example, the current unemployment rate is below 5%, down from almost 10% in 2010 following the financial crisis. As we look back on Obama and follow the current White House, data is the only nonpartisian measure of our society available to us and will be able to objectively indicate certain measures of success or failure. I compiled a series of datasets on various topics during the Obama years (and in some cases earlier to provide context) and used Plotly to display them; hover over the graphs for specific values. Notably, in National trends the country has emerged from the financial crisis with 4.6% unemployment, 13.5% living in poverty, and 10.5% still without health insurance (see graphs below).
