Sacred Valley

Jul 23 2016

After Machu Picchu I remained in Cusco to see the ruins that surrounded the city. Traveling north one day with an English speaking tour I went to the Sacred Valley to see the Inca ruins of Pisac and Ollantaytambo. The next day I went south with a Spanish speaking tour to the Valle del Sur to see the agricultural terraces of Tipón which were irrigated by a series of stone aqueducts fed by a spring so well engineered that they still function today.


On my last day in Cusco I went to the ruins around the city that I had missed. Given that I had all day I decided to go by horse since I had never ridden a horse before and it was extremely easy with the myriad of agencies lining the Plaza de Armas. It was just me and a mother and child on this tour led by our horseman who was a young man that could control the horses mostly with clicking directions from his mouth. I chatted with the woman whose name I learned was Carmela while she rode with her daughter, Valentina, either in her arms or slung on her back in a traditional Peruvian textile.
