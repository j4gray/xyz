Packing for Peru

Jun 30 2016

I am preparing for a trip to Peru where I will complete a lifelong dream of climbing Machu Picchu. Being in high altitude in a different hemisphere requires preparation and as I secure some diamox (for altitude sickness) and antibiotics (in case of emergency) along with the usual smorgasboard of first aid (fresh benadryl, moleskin, etc.) I can reflect on how lucky I am to be unconcerned with disease.


This reflection comes in step with the 2016 Olympics being held in Rio amid various health concerns and I wanted to pay respect to those individuals, athletes or actors, whose business depends on their bodily health. The Zika virus is a unique health concern because while mosquito borne illnesses have been plaguing humans for centuries, the risks associated with pregnacy pose a significant concern to future generations where this virus is breeding. While I admire those who devote their lives to their physical health I hope that the athletes dropping out of the Rio 2016 Olympics calls attention to the very real risk we face with the Zika virus and draws funding to eradicate it.
