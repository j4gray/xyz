Lima

Jul 9 2016

I arrived in Lima at 6AM and after checking into a hostel I headed out into the neighborhood of Miraflores to stretch my legs after the long flight. The neighborhood is on the beach and I quickly found the ocean and the winter day provided a cool breeze that did not deter surfers from going out in wetsuits. The parks lining the beach in Miraflores are gorgeous and I used a Foder's travel guide to educate myself as I walked along the coast stopping perioidcally to read it and orient myself using a combination of my phone's offline GPS and a compass that I relied to offer quick and trustworthy headings.


I made no efforts to hide my identiy as a tourist, dressed in my gray treking pants, hiking boots, and rain jacket, and took a cab to the historic center of the city where the Presidential palace and Catholic Cathedral lined the Plaza de Armas. I am already enjoying my conversational Spanish skills as all the locals I have interacted with so far have been pleasantly surpised with my competancy and all too happy to speak slowly and annunciate while offering information and suggestions for my trip.


Sitting in the Plaza de Armas a group of young girls approach me and politely ask if they can interview me because they have a school project to interview "una extranjera en ingles." I smiled and said "Pues, soy extranjera." They asked me about my impressions of Peru and I told them it was my first day but I was excited to eat ceviche and see the ancient history.
