Election Coverage

Oct 27 2016


It is safe to say that this election is unlike any other in history and I've found myself sending out so many links of bizarre interviews and gut busting comedy spots that I figured I would aggregate them all in one place in case anyone wants to surf through the seemingly parallel reality that is this election.

CNN


Panel on Access Hollywood tape (10/8/16)


Highlights: Ana Navarro explodes at Scottie Nell Hughes who asks her to stop saying pussey on the air at 9:20 saying "Don't tell me you're offended when I say pussey but you're not offended when Donald Trump says it."



Brianna Keilar & Ben Carson(10/11/16)


Highlights: At 7:30 Brianna Keilar asks Ben Carson if he's ever heard language like what Trump was recorded saying to Billy Bush and he says "Of course" and that he's surprised she hasn't heard it. When she says she hasn't (and knows a lot of people who haven't) he responds, "Well, maybe that's the problem."





Fox News


Donald Trump after the First Debate (9/27/16)


Highlights: At 7:06 he starts talking about Alicia Machado and the reaction of the Fox reporters is priceless, they have no idea how to respond to his comments on her gaining "a trememndous amount of weight." Then at 9:50 he goes in on his "bum" microphone, which he blames for Hillary's superior performance; once again the reporters are struck speechless. He then goes on to blame the microphone for his nonstop sniffling during the debate at 12:50.



Megyn Kelly & Mike Huckabee (10/10/16)


Highlights: At 5:30 Huckabee compares Trump to Captain Quint from Jaws and Hillary to the shark... Megyn points out that Captain Quint gets eaten by the shark at the end of the movie.



Megyn Kelly & Newt Gingrich (10/25/16)


Highlights: Megyn asks about the concern surrounding the accusations leveled against Trump about being a sexual predator and when Newt explodes she calmly says at 5:14, "I think that your defensiveness on this may speak volumes sir." Newt gets even more worked up and concludes by telling Megyn that she is "facinated with sex" at 6:18.





Kellyanne Conway


I have a strange facination with Kellyanne Conway and I have ever since she became Trump's campaign manager. While I do not support her candidate, I am impressed by how well she has been handling him and diffusing all of the ridiculous stuff he says with a smile on her face. She is clearly very intelligent, which only adds to my fascination as she is forced to defend some of the most ignorant and controversial statements ever made by someone running for public office.


Trump's Debate Prep (9/28/16)


Highlights: "At the end of the debate after she tried to rough him up over a couple commments he's made over 25 years or so with respect to women, he said, 'You know I came prepared to say some rough stuff but I won't do it because your family is here and your daughter is here, but it's not nice that you're running hundreds of millions of dollars in negative ads against me'"



Post-debate Spin (10/9/16)


Highlights: In response to the CNN poll that said 57% of people said Hillary won the debate she asserted "I watched a different debate."



Anderson Cooper on Settling Lawsuits (10/11/16)


Highlights: At 7:05 Kellyanne tries to call Bill Clinton out for settling a lawsuit with Paula Jones and Anderson Cooper turns right around and asks if she thinks that settling is an admission of guilt, where does that leave Donald Trump? He has settled numerous lawsuits including but not limited to the lawsuit in 1973 concerning racial discrimination in his housing units.



Jake Tapper on Rigged Elections (10/23/16)


Highlights: At 5:45 Tapper plays a clip of Kellyanne herself (when she was still working for Ted Cruz) calling Trump out during the primaries for "whining" about a rigged and corrupt system.



SNL: "A Day Off" with Kellyanne Conway (10/8/16)


Highlights: Once again Kate McKinnon nails the impression, this time of Trump's campaign manager Kellyanne Conway trying to deal with the never ending storm of media spin required to diffuse Trump's comments.





Saturday Night Live Debates


Easily the best parody of this election. Kate McKinnon is (in my opinion) SNL's best asset right now and when you add Alec Baldwin as Donald Trump the result is comedic dynamite.


First Debate (10/1/16)


Highlights: 3:50 - "He hasn't released his tax returns, which means he's either not that rich-"
            "WRONG"
            "Not that charitable-"
            "WRONG"
            "Or he's never paid taxes in his life-"
            "WARMER"



Second Debate (10/15/16)


Highlights: 3:15 - "Listen, what I said is nothing compared to what Bill Clinton has done, OK, he has abused women and Martha, Anderson - hold onto your nips and your nuts because 4 of those women are here tonight. 4 of them."
            "Wait, I'm sorry, who's here? Mistresses? Bill, how could you? How will I go on with the debate? No, I'll never be able to remember my facts and figures now! Ohhh Donald noooo - GET REAL! I'm made of steel, this is nothing. Hi girls!"



Third Debate (10/22/16)


Highlights: 7:40 - "Frankly, this whole thing is rigged. Even the media; everyday I turn on the news and all of the newscasters are making me look so bad."
      "And how are we doing that?"
      "By taking all of the things I say and all of the things I do and putting them on TV."





Seth Meyers


Mike Pence Pretends Donald Trump isn't his Running Mate (10/5/16)


Highlights: Starting at 8:00, Seth nails Pence on his denial of things Donald Trump is on tape saying. Then he overlays those tapes with Pence's signature squinty head shake culminating in Pence denying what he himself has said about Putin.



The Trump Tape and Debate Fallout (10/10/16)


Highlights: In response to Corey Lewandowski saying Trump speaks "from the heart... the way many times people talk around their dining room table," Seth said, "Yeah that's right, he was talking to Billy Bush the way people talk around the dining room table... Honey this meatloaf is fantastic, I'm gonna move on it like a bitch."
