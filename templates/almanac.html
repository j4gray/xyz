Enceladus is a moon of Saturn that is approximately 500 km (310 mi) in diameter that is entirely covered by ice, making it an extremely reflective body in the solar system, and has a subsurface ocean. This was confirmed by observations of the large plumes spewing from the sourhtern polar surface, determined to be mostly water, with a small amount of hydrogen molecutles. The exhaust from these jets (250 kg of water every second) is shot out at up to 2,189 km/h (1,360 mph), immediatley freezes in space, and is caputured by Saturn's gravity, forming the E Ring.

Harry Kendall Thaw (1871-1947) was a multimillionaire from Pittsburgh who moved to New York at the turn of the century and became obsessed with the renowned archietect Standford White. On the night of June 25, 1906, Thaw murdered White on the rooftop of Madison Square Garden in front of hundreds of witnesses with a revolver at close range. He was later was later found not guilty after two murder trials.

Does a horse lift all four feet at any point when it's galloping? In order to find out Eadweard Muybridge took 24 photographs in rapid succession of a galloping horse, titled Sallie Gardner at a Gallop, that eventually shown on a zoopraxiscope in 1878 around the country, forshadowing the eventual explosion of motion pictures. The pictures showed clearly there is a point when the legs are pulled in that all four feet are off the ground.

This year marks a decade that Priness Diana was killed in a car crash in Paris, on the night of August 31st.

Cassini is a probe that was sent to Saturn in 1997 as a joint effort by NASA, The European Space Agenecy and the Italian Space Agency. It arrived in 2004 and has been surverying Saturn ever since. On September 15 2017, it will end it's final mission of taking photos within Saturn's rings before entering the atmosphere and burning up.

Puerto Rico is the one of the largest exporters of pharmaceuticals and medical devices in the world and accounts for more than half of all manufacturing on the island. Hurricane Maria, while reeking havoc on the US territory and it’s 3.3 million residents, devastatingly crippled the electrical grid. Most homes and factories are relying on diesel generators for power causing shortages of some drugs and medical products like saline solution.

Cagras delusion is a psychiatric disorder in which a person holds a delusion that a friend, spouse, parent, or other close family member has been replace by an identical imposter. It is named after Joseph Capgras who first described the disorder in 1923 affecting a French woman known as "Madame Macabre."

Ann Lowe designed Jackie's dress when she married JFK; she was one of the most famous and desired designers among high society from the 1920s to the 1960s. She was the first African American to become a famous fashion designer and her name missing from the vernacular of fashion is a huge shame.

The Waffle House Index is an informal metric used by FEMA to determine the effect of a storm and the likely scale of assistance required for disaster recovery: "if you get there and the Waffle House is closed? That's really bad"

Madam C. J. Walker was the wealthiest African American businesswoman and wealthiest self made woman in America at the time of her death in 1919. Her fortune, estimated at $600,000, came from development and marketing of a line of cosmetics and hair care products for black women. She was well known for her philanthropy and activism including with the YMCA and the NAACP

The word Mamihlapinatapai, from the Yaghan language of Tierra del Fuego, is listed in the Guinness Book of World Records as the "most succinct word." It translates as "a look shared by two people, each wishing that the other would initiate somethign that they both desire but which neither wants to begin."

The House of Habsburg was one of the most influential royal houses of Europe, occupying the throne of the Holy Roman Empire from 1438 until 1740. They consolidated power through inter-familial marriages often marrying first cousins or uncles and nieces. This eventually lead to such a small gene pool that the last of the Spanish line, Charles II, was disabled from birth and had a genome 'comparable to taht of a child born to a brother and sister.'

Mount Everest is probably my brother's favorite Wikipedia page. He loves the 16-part section on History of Expeditions, which provides an overview and links to all the famous mountaineering disasters that have occured on Everest. There is also a good section on the commercialization of Everest and what is has meant for the local ecosystem and economy.

A Chewbacca defense is a legal strategy in which a criminal defense lawyer tries to confuse the jury instead of refuting the case of the prosecutor, which comes from an episode of South park that satirizes the O. J. Simpson murder trial.

Charles V ruled from 1368 (when he was 11) until his death in 1422, and was known as both le Bien-Aimé (the beloved) and le Fol (the mad). He suffered from mental illness that caused him to be extremely paranoid and belive he was made of glass.

The anglerfish is well-known because of the distinctive luminescent 'lure' that grows from it's head, which is an organ called the esca. Less well known is the extreme 'sexual dimorphism' between males and females in the reproductive process. Males are significally smaller than females and will die if they cannot attach themselves to a female and live parasitically off of them.

Xenotransplantation is the transplantation of living cells, tissues, or organs from one species to another. Contrast with allotransplantation, which invloves an individual of the same species, or autotransplantation, which is from one part of the body to another in the same person. The first attempt at xenotransplantation was in 1905 using slices of rabbit kidney to treat a child with renal insufficiency.

Omphaloskepsis or 'naval gazing' is contemplation of one's naval as an aid to meditation. Today, it has come to mean self-absorbed pursuits of limited value.

The word 'empanada' comes from the Spanish 'empanar' meaning 'enbreaded' (pan being bread) and they can be fried or baked and stuffed with anything you can imagine. One of my favorite parts about visiting Latin America is discovering what the local empanada variety is. Wikpedia provides a great guide by country and region
